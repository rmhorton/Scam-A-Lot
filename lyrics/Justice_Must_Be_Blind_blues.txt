Justice Must Be Blind (Blues Version)


Blues-gospel protest song with Hammond organ, smoky blues guitar, preacher vocals, and call-and-response choir. Southern revival-meeting atmosphere exposing courtroom corruption and hypocrisy. Slow build from solemn testimony to righteous communal outrage. Raw Southern soul and swamp blues energy. Fade out on repeated gospel refrain.

INTRO — SPOKEN PREACHER]

Brothers and sisters…
Can I get a witness?

They carved “Equal Justice Under Law”
Right there in the marble stone…
But somehow the law gets weaker
The richer you are.

[MUSIC ENTERS — SLOW BLUES GOSPEL GROOVE]

[VERSE 1]

A county judge took hunting trips
And they ran him out of town
Another took consulting fees
They dragged his reputation down

But billionaires buy dinners
With the mighty and the wise
And somehow every ethics rule
Just quietly dies

[CHOIR]

Mmmmmm…
Tell the truth now…

[PRE-CHORUS]

They say the law is sacred
They say the court is pure
But the richer that the scandal gets
The less they seem so sure

[CHORUS]

Justice must be blind
That’s what the courthouse said
But somehow she keeps recognizing
The wealthy and well-bred

Justice must be blind
Can I get amen?
Poor folks face the full damn law
Rich folks golf with friends

[VERSE 2]

A public defender’s client
Lost his children and his home
While donors drank expensive wine
With people wearing robes

They call it “appearance impropriety”
That holy courtroom phrase
But appearances get flexible
For power, wealth, and praise

[CHOIR]

Flexible!
Flexible!
Lord have mercy…

[VERSE 3]

They built the courthouse like a temple
Tall columns white as snow
But money walks through side doors
That regular people never know

One law for the working people
One law for the well-connected few
And every smiling television expert
Pretends this all is new

[PRE-CHORUS]

They say “respect the process”
They say “protect the court”
Funny how “integrity” gets soft
When billionaires get caught

[CHORUS — BIGGER]

Justice must be blind
That’s what the marble reads
But somehow she keeps noticing
Who has power, wealth, and deeds

Justice must be blind
Can I get release?
Justice wears a blindfold
But money lets her see

[BRIDGE — PREACHER BUILD]

Now wait just a minute…

You mean to tell me
A small-town judge gets ruined
For favors and free vacations…

But private jets and luxury gifts
For the powerful?
That’s just networking?

[CHOIR]

THAT AIN’T JUSTICE!

[SPOKEN]

No no…
That’s membership.

[MUSIC DROPS TO ORGAN + CLAPS]

[FINAL CHORUS — CALL AND RESPONSE]

[SINGER]

Who gets punished?

[CHOIR]

Ordinary people!

[SINGER]

Who gets mercy?

[CHOIR]

Friends of power!

[SINGER]

What they call corruption—

[CHOIR]

Depends who’s taking checks!

[SINGER]

What they call justice—

[CHOIR]

Depends who’s writing laws!

[SINGER + CHOIR]

Justice must be blind
That’s what the good book says
But somehow she keeps bowing down
To money, power, praise

Justice must be blind
Can I get amen?
There’s one law for the poor folks
Another for their friends

[OUTRO — REPEATED]

Justice must be blind…
Justice must be blind…
