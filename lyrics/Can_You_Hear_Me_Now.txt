
Late-night atmospheric Americana ballad with intimate female vocals, sparse electric guitar, soft piano, warm analog synth textures, and subtle radio-static ambience. Conversational, emotionally vulnerable delivery with restrained dynamics and minimal percussion. Feels like a lonely pirate-radio broadcast after midnight: exhausted, sincere, melancholic, and quietly hopeful. Focus on lyrical clarity and human intimacy rather than theatrical performance or anthem energy.

CAN YOU HEAR ME NOW?

(Late-night pirate radio ballad)

In somebody’s empty room
The television’s glowing blue
Everybody’s talking loud
Nobody hears what’s true

Everybody sounds cruel online
Meaner than they really are
But I’ve seen strangers
Help each other push dead cars
In parking lots

And I don’t think we were built
To hear ten thousand strangers every day
And call that knowing people
Then wonder why we’re afraid

CHORUS

Can you hear me now?
Through the chorus of machines
There’s so much shouting everywhere
Nobody’s listening

Can you hear me now?
Through the headlines and the fear
Another tragedy every hour
Nobody sheds a tear


⸻

I met a woman at a diner
Near the interstate last spring
She said she hadn’t spoken to her brother
Since before quarantine

But she still reads every post he writes
And hates him more and more
Then stared into her coffee cup
And said:

“I don’t even know if he’s real anymore.”

⸻

Sometimes I forget what people sound like
Without an audience
I catch myself rehearsing conversations
Like somebody’s grading every utterance

Everybody’s broad-casting
Nobody says it hurts
Just headlines flying back and forth
Like prayers nobody heard

CHORUS

Can you hear me now?
Through the static and the scroll
Are you doing your own research
Or falling down a rabbit hole?

Can you hear me now?
Do you ever go out for a walk?
Everybody's shouting
But can't remember how to talk


⸻

My roommate studied sociology
Yellow highlighter on every page
She said humans weren’t designed to carry
So many voices, this much rage

She could explain exactly why
People perform themselves online
Then spend an hour rewriting a brief reply
Like strangers held her life

In their hands

Mid-semester her side of the room
Went empty suddenly
Sometimes I still start sentences
Like she’s sitting in the passenger seat

I was numb far longer than I knew
Everybody felt far away
Then little things started breaking through...

⸻

I saw two men stop screaming politics
To help an old man on the ice
Saw a lady tell a homeless woman
That her hair looked nice

Like she really meant it

I don’t think the world is hopeless
Not completely, anyway
I still see people choosing kindness
In a hundred quiet ways

Just people still reaching for each other
In the middle of the noise

FINAL CHORUS

Can you hear me now?
Is the signal breaking through?
Somewhere in the midnight static
Somebody’s listening too

Can you hear me now?
You have the facts but do you understand?
You found the answer, did you find the truth?
Did you build a house upon the sand?

Can you hear me now?
Can you see what life is all about?
Through the constant little judgments
Are you paralyzed with doubt?

Can you hear me now?
Then maybe we’re still here
Maybe all these quiet human voices
Are stronger than our fear

Can you hear me now?
You don’t have to say a word
I just need to know
Somebody heard.
