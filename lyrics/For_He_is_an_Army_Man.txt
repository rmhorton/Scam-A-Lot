Satirical Gilbert and Sullivan operetta in the style of a triumphant Victorian naval anthem. Proud male chorus, stately comic operetta vocals, brass band, snare drums, piccolo flourishes, theatrical orchestration, exaggerated patriotic grandeur, cheerful militaristic pomp, crisp enunciation, lively comic orchestra, increasingly unsettling nationalist enthusiasm, dark political satire beneath an upbeat ceremonial tone.

FOR HE IS AN ARMY MAN

(to the tune of “For He Is an Englishman” from Gilbert and Sullivan’s H.M.S. Pinafore)

He is an Army Man!

[Verse 1: Crusader warrior-bro]

He is an Army Man!
For he himself has said it
And it’s greatly to his credit
That he is an Army Man!

Oh, a warrior’s life is glorious
When televised victorious!
Now every tragedy
Demands lethality!

[Verse 2: Escalation and incentives]

When diplomats are cautioning
He says that peace is softening
The spirit of the land!
For he is an Army Man!

The pundits all adore him
The donors all implore him
To rise and take command!
He is their Army Man!

Negotiation’s terrible
But shock and awe is shareable
When shot from a drone cam!
For war’s a business plan!

[Verse 3: Endless war and class asymmetry]

While boardrooms toast another year
The wealthy sons stay safely clear
Whenever wars are planned!
And poorer boys are called to stand!

A war can fail for years on end
Yet still produce dividends
While flags across the land
Declare he is an Army Man!
